# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as members, contributors, and leaders pledge to make participation in our community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our Standards

### Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment for our community include:

- **Being Respectful**: Treating all community members with respect and kindness
- **Being Inclusive**: Welcoming newcomers and helping them feel part of the community
- **Being Constructive**: Providing helpful feedback and engaging in productive discussions
- **Being Professional**: Maintaining professional conduct in all interactions
- **Being Patient**: Understanding that everyone has different skill levels and learning paces
- **Being Collaborative**: Working together towards common goals
- **Giving Credit**: Acknowledging others' contributions and ideas
- **Being Open to Learning**: Accepting feedback graciously and continuously improving

### Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- **Harassment**: Any form of harassment, including but not limited to:
  - Trolling, insulting, or derogatory comments
  - Personal or political attacks
  - Public or private harassment
  - Sexual harassment or unwelcome sexual attention
- **Discrimination**: Discriminatory language or actions based on personal characteristics
- **Spam**: Posting irrelevant content or excessive self-promotion
- **Doxxing**: Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- **Impersonation**: Pretending to be someone else or misrepresenting credentials
- **Violation of Privacy**: Sharing private communications without consent
- **Disruption**: Deliberately disrupting discussions or community activities
- **Bad Faith**: Engaging in arguments or discussions without genuine intent to contribute

## Community-Specific Guidelines

### For Technical Discussions
- **Be Patient with Beginners**: Remember that everyone was a beginner once
- **Provide Constructive Feedback**: Focus on the code/idea, not the person
- **Share Knowledge Freely**: Help others learn and grow
- **Avoid Gatekeeping**: Don't discourage people based on their current skill level
- **Acknowledge Different Approaches**: Recognize that there are often multiple valid solutions

### For Blog Contributions
- **Original Content**: Ensure your content is original or properly attributed
- **Factual Accuracy**: Verify technical information before publishing
- **Inclusive Language**: Use language that is accessible to diverse audiences
- **Cultural Sensitivity**: Be mindful of cultural differences within the Indian developer community

### For Code Contributions
- **Follow Project Standards**: Adhere to coding standards and guidelines
- **Test Your Code**: Ensure your contributions work as expected
- **Document Changes**: Provide clear descriptions of what your code does
- **Respect Existing Architecture**: Understand the project structure before making changes

## Enforcement Responsibilities

Community leaders and maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing our standards of acceptable behavior and will take appropriate and fair corrective action in response to any behavior that they deem inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Community leaders have the right and responsibility to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, wiki edits, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned to this Code of Conduct, and will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when appropriate.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all community spaces, and also applies when an individual is officially representing the community in public spaces. Examples of representing our community include:

- Using an official email address
- Posting via an official social media account
- Acting as an appointed representative at an online or offline event
- Contributing to project repositories
- Participating in project discussions, issues, or pull requests

## Reporting Guidelines

### How to Report
If you experience or witness unacceptable behavior, or have any other concerns, please report it by:

1. **GitHub Issues**: For public incidents that aren't sensitive
2. **Private Contact**: For sensitive matters, contact project maintainers directly
3. **GitHub's Community Guidelines**: Report serious violations to GitHub directly

### What to Include in Your Report
When reporting an incident, please include:

- **Description**: What happened?
- **Context**: Where and when did it occur?
- **People Involved**: Who was involved? (usernames if known)
- **Evidence**: Links to comments, screenshots, etc. (if applicable)
- **Impact**: How did this affect you or the community?
- **Previous Incidents**: Any related previous incidents

### Confidentiality
All reports will be handled with discretion. We respect the privacy of those who report incidents and will only share information on a need-to-know basis for investigation and resolution purposes.

## Enforcement Guidelines

Community leaders will follow these Community Impact Guidelines in determining the consequences for any action they deem in violation of this Code of Conduct:

### 1. Correction
**Community Impact**: Use of inappropriate language or other behavior deemed unprofessional or unwelcome in the community.

**Consequence**: A private, written warning from community leaders, providing clarity around the nature of the violation and an explanation of why the behavior was inappropriate. A public apology may be requested.

### 2. Warning
**Community Impact**: A violation through a single incident or series of actions.

**Consequence**: A warning with consequences for continued behavior. No interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, for a specified period of time. This includes avoiding interactions in community spaces as well as external channels like social media. Violating these terms may lead to a temporary or permanent ban.

### 3. Temporary Ban
**Community Impact**: A serious violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior.

**Consequence**: A temporary ban from any sort of interaction or public communication with the community for a specified period of time. No public or private interaction with the people involved, including unsolicited interaction with those enforcing the Code of Conduct, is allowed during this period. Violating these terms may lead to a permanent ban.

### 4. Permanent Ban
**Community Impact**: Demonstrating a pattern of violation of community standards, including sustained inappropriate behavior, harassment of an individual, or aggression toward or disparagement of classes of individuals.

**Consequence**: A permanent ban from any sort of public interaction within the community.

## Appeals Process

If you believe you have been falsely or unfairly accused of violating this Code of Conduct, you may appeal the decision by:

1. **Contacting Maintainers**: Reach out to project maintainers with your concerns
2. **Providing Evidence**: Present any evidence that supports your position
3. **Requesting Review**: Ask for a review of the decision by uninvolved community leaders

Appeals will be reviewed fairly and in a timely manner.

## Special Considerations for Our Community

### Cultural Diversity
Our community includes developers from across India and beyond, representing diverse:
- **Languages**: Hindi, English, Tamil, Bengali, and many others
- **Regions**: Urban and rural areas with different access to resources
- **Economic Backgrounds**: Various socio-economic situations
- **Educational Backgrounds**: Self-taught developers, bootcamp graduates, and formal CS students

We celebrate this diversity and expect all members to be respectful of these differences.

### Skill Level Inclusion
We welcome developers at all levels:
- **Beginners**: Those just starting their coding journey
- **Students**: Those currently learning through formal education
- **Career Changers**: Professionals transitioning to technology
- **Experienced Developers**: Industry veterans sharing their knowledge
- **Open Source Contributors**: Those contributing to various projects

Everyone's perspective and contribution is valuable.
